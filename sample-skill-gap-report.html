Q5.  Got it. Do you actively build your professional presence outside your day-to-day role — writing, speaking, or any kind of thought leadership?

Q6.  That's more common than you'd think! How comfortable are you presenting a new editorial idea or initiative to your leadership team?

I usually do this with a custom find-replace sequence I've built up over the years. It's pretty systematic at this point.
Q8.  That's an impressive level of precision — clearly your core skill. Last question: where do you see yourself in 12 months, and what's the one skill you most want to develop?
I'd love to be in a Senior Editorial Lead role — managing a small team and having more influence over the content strategy. The skill I most want to develop is probably strategic communication — being able to articulate ideas clearly to leadership and get buy-in. I also want to get better at planning and using data to back editorial decisions.
